Missions FAQ

Frequently asked questions by students about missions.

According to the Joshua Project: "An unreached or least-reached people is a people group among which there is no indigenous community of believing Christians with adequate numbers and resources to evangelize this people group without outside assistance." This is often metrically defined by a group that has less than 2% Evangelical Christians.

Always start with prayer! Ask God to shape your heart to be like his as you use tools like the Joshua project to learn about and pray for the world. Then, connect with local organizations and serve or chat about missions with organization leaders.

Everyone is called to make disciples (Matthew 28:19-20), but not everyone is called to be a long-term cross-cultural missionary. However, everyone can be involved in missions by praying, giving, and going short-term or long-term to serve in missions. It does nto matter what background field you are from there are people around you that need to be loved, and you can offer that as an engineer, a teacher, waitor, or any other profession.

There are people who need the gospel everywhere. Jesus told his disciples to be witnesses locally, domestically, and internationally. Here in Auburn there are poele that don't know Jesus, and we have the opportunity to share the gospel with them first before moving on to other areas.
